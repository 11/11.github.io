Links

As a developer, I too go out to the interwebs to find good resources to learn from. To pay tribute to those who have helped me over the years, this page is designed for those of you that are eager to captivate your curious minds.

  • Youtube Links
  • Web Links
  • Game Development Links

Youtube Links

  • 3Brown1Blue is one of the best math related Youtube channels on the internet. If you have never seen or heard of this channel, do yourself a favor and check it out.
  • DevTips is there for all of your front-end needs.
  • Web Concepts talks about computer science networking esq topics.
  • Seth Bling is entertaining video game enthusiast who enjoys messing with hardware and breaking video games.
  • thenewboston is how I originally learned to code back in 7th grade. Check this guy out!
  • Sesh Venugopaul was a computer science professor of mine and has great data structure videos.
  • Innuendo Studios is a channel that has created his own niche. Just listen to this guy - everything he says is wise and thought provoking.

Web Links

  • Systems Programming is how I caught up in class if I ever missed a Systems Programming lecture.
  • Learn You a Haskell for a Great Good! is the best Haskell web book out there, and its free
  • Explained Visually is the way everyone should learn. Seriously check out this website.

Game Development Links

  • AI & Games is exactly what it sounds like.
  • Game Design & Development Tools, may I recommend the gird based path finding article.
  • Every Frame a Painting doesn't talk about video games, but gives great advice about storytelling through contrasting movies and directors.
  • Extra Credits if you have wanted to become an indie game developer, go here.
  • Classic Game Junkie is an up and coming Youtube content creator. Knows a lot about the history of games and gives true insight to the development process of a game, via their glitches.
