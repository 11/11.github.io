Act Out

Posted on June 26th, 2018

Don't be timid to act out

  1. Act Out

No one said that you have to be the person that were you five minutes ago. No one said that you can’t try to stand out. No one is telling you to do anything, it’s only you agreeing with the people that tell you things.

Let me share something with you. When I was six I was placed into remedial classes because I read twice as slow. When I was seven, I went to speech classes because I couldn’t pronounce my T’s without an H. When I was in fifth grade, it was the first and only time I ever got straight A’s. When I was in seventh grade, I failed three courses. When I was in 8th grade, I failed 5 courses. When I hit high school, I was back in remedial classes to compensate for my performance. When I took the SAT i got a 1250/2400 … to only get a 1230/2400 after studying. When I applied to college, my guidance counselor told me, “students with your grades end up at a gas station.” When I applied to internships I was rejected 236 times - not one gave me an interview.

My entire academic career was shit. I was considered a dud, a failure, an example of what not to do. If I had actually believed that, I wouldn’t be where I am right now. School, no matter how many people pride themselves on their academic accomplishments, is for the mindless souls who’d rather follow directions than think for themselves. Sure, I might not know the fiftteenth president from rote memorization, but I know how to think for myself.

When I come into work, I see people from a variety of backgrounds, yet the one commonality underneath it all is a good student who had the opportunity and or the privilege to attend a great school all the while encouraged by their family to do well. Knowing this, I identify with no one. No one knows what it was like to have to pull your thirty-fifth all nighter during the semester because you barely read a text that is slightly above a high school reading level.

I’m sitting here writing this because I was told by my friends that I’m incompetent. I’m writing this because everyday I think about these thoughts, and I’m tired of being someone who blends into your day to day. I’m tired of being seen as someone who doesn’t deserve your attention.
